About

My name is Shiloh Williams and I use they/them pronouns. I currently work as the Acting Head of the Digital Scholarship Lab and the Map, Data & GIS Library at Brock University, where I provide instructional and research support for data and GIS on campus. Prior to that, I was the Data Visualization and GIS Librarian at the University of Texas at Arlington.

I have also worked at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) and the Center for the Study of Political Graphics, where I analyzed and visualized collections data. I also hold a Masters degree in Library and Information Science from UCLA, where I studied the technical and theoretical aspects of data science.

Outside of work, I enjoy visiting museums and galleries; reading books; listening to music in big headphones; experimenting with analog photography (usually with a Minolta X-700, Polaroid OneStep, or Holga 120N); cooking and baking with a stand mixer I got off Kijiji; playing JRPGs like Final Fantasy and Shin Megami Tensei; and, when it’s warm enough, going on walks and exploring Toronto’s public transit system.
